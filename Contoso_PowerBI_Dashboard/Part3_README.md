# 👥 Contoso Retail — Customer Analysis Dashboard

## 📌 Overview
This page analyses customer behaviour, value, and contribution to overall business performance. It focuses on how the customer base is evolving over time, how much value each customer generates, and whether revenue is concentrated among a small group of high-value customers.

---

## 🎯 Purpose of This Page

This page answers the following business questions:

- Are we gaining more customers over time?  
- Is each customer becoming more or less valuable?  
- Is revenue driven by many customers or a small group?  
- Who are the top-performing customers?  

---

## 📊 Key Metrics (Top KPIs)

- **Customers** → Total number of distinct customers  
- **Sales per Customer** → Average revenue per customer  
- **Profit per Customer** → Average profit per customer  
- **Orders** → Total number of transactions  

These KPIs provide a high-level view of customer growth, value, and activity.

---

## 📈 Customer Trends Over Time

### 🔹 Customers Over Time (Area Chart)
- Tracks number of customers using `YearMonth`  
- Highlights growth trends and fluctuations in acquisition  

### 🔹 Sales per Customer Over Time (Line Chart)
- Tracks average revenue per customer over time  
- Shows whether customer value is increasing or decreasing

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- **Customer base is growing over time**  
  → Indicates successful acquisition  

- **Sales per customer is declining**  
  → Customers are becoming less valuable on average  

- **Revenue is concentrated among a small group of customers**  
  → A few customers drive a large portion of revenue  

- **Growth is volume-driven rather than value-driven**  
  → More customers, but lower average contribution  

---

## 📌 Summary

The business is acquiring more customers, but each customer is generating less value on average. At the same time, a relatively small group of customers contributes a large share of total revenue. This suggests an opportunity to improve customer value through better targeting, retention strategies, and upselling, rather than relying solely on acquiring more customers.
